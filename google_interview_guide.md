## 🧠 Will 350 Problems Be Enough to Crack Google?

**Yes, absolutely.** Here's why:

- Google doesn't test obscure algorithms. They test **pattern recognition** and **problem decomposition**.
- Most Google questions map to **15-20 core patterns** (sliding window, BFS/DFS, DP states, etc.).
- 350 problems covering all patterns with deep understanding beats 1000 problems done superficially.
- Many successful Google candidates solved **200-300 problems** but understood them deeply.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> The key is **depth over quantity**. For every problem, understand WHY the approach works, not just HOW.

## 🔥 Top 15 Expert Tips

### Problem-Solving Strategy
1. **Pattern Recognition > Memorization** — Learn 15-20 core patterns. Every Google question maps to one or more.
2. **Always Start with Brute Force** — State brute force first, analyze complexity, then optimize. Shows structured thinking.
3. **Think Out Loud** — Google explicitly scores communication. Narrate: "I'm considering a hashmap because we need O(1) lookups..."
4. **Master Time & Space Complexity** — For EVERY solution, know exact Big-O. Google always asks "Can you do better?"
5. **Write Clean Code** — Meaningful variable names, modular functions, edge case handling. Google's code quality bar is very high.

### Preparation Strategy
6. **Do Each Problem 3 Times** — First attempt → study solution → redo after 3 days (spaced repetition).
7. **Track Weak Areas** — Maintain a spreadsheet. Fail a graph problem? Do 5 more graph problems before moving on.
8. **Time Yourself** — Medium: 20-25 min. Hard: 35-40 min. Real interviews give ~45 min total.
9. **Practice Without IDE** — Google interviews use Google Docs or whiteboard. No autocomplete, no syntax highlighting.
10. **Weekly Mock Interviews** — Use Pramp, Interviewing.io, or study partners. Simulate real pressure.

### Google-Specific Tips
11. **Google Loves Graph Problems** — BFS/DFS, shortest path, topological sort, union-find are tested disproportionately.
12. **Google Loves DP** — Especially 2D DP, interval DP, and DP on trees/graphs. Don't skip hard DP.
13. **System Design (L4+)** — Prepare by studying "Designing Data-Intensive Applications" and designing Gmail, Maps, YouTube.
14. **Googleyness & Leadership** — Prepare 5-6 STAR stories about teamwork, conflict, ambiguity, and impact.
15. **Get a Referral** — Reach out to Google engineers on LinkedIn. Referrals significantly increase callback rates.
